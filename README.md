YoY tells that production averagely changed through years 4.93%. the problem is that there are some big outliers, so I made one more measure CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) which shows if production changed constantly the same every year to go from first year of production (5.840,757k) to last year production (17.020,549k) it would be growth in production of 2.16% every year.



**Sources of Corn Supply** - main source of corn in period 1975.-2025. (85,48%) is corn production. Second most important source is the stock from the previous year, and on third place is import which is very small amount and has minimal impact on corn stocks. **What we can conclude from this is that the USA mostly relay on domestic production, which makes them highly sensitive to drops in production.**



**Use Distribution** - corn is primarily used for consumption-related purposes, on second place is Industrial use with 31,97% of total use , third place takes export with 19,03% and a small portion of total stocks goes to seed use. **What is important here is that the USA used one third of whole corn stocks on industry, which indicates that focus on industrial processing and in that way they create more value, instead of focusing on exporting raw product.**



**Production by Year** - chart clearly shows that in period from 1975. to 2025. corn production had growing trend. Some years were significantly low, for example: 1983., 1988., 1993., 2012.

But considering last 20 years, we can say that production is constantly rising and that only 2012. was bad year for production. **From trend line we can conclude that production will continue to moderately grow.**



This brings us to the **Trend od Corn Usage by Categories**. If we observe export and food usage, we can agree that they vary a little bit, but not significantly, and overall they have grown slightly over time. Seed use has grown over time which is normal since that production has the same trend.

Now, what really is interesting is a line which shows industrial use. We clearly see that line started rising in 2000. and in the next 10 years that quantity doubled. **This explains rising in production from '00.**



Further, data says that **the worst years** in corn production were during '70.-'90., and that **the most successful years** were in the last 10 years. This clearly shows us that **production improved** a lot and that production is **stable and growing**.



**Relation between Corn Production, Total use and Stocks at the and of the year** - of course as we already established production notes growth, and consumption follows that trend. But ending stocks does not follow this trend. Ending stocks vary a bit, we can see that sometimes they were a bit lower and that is a case for years when there was a drop in production, which is normal. Overall ending stock have some constant level end they are not rising. **This indicated that growth in production comes from growing needs in industry.**



When it comes to **Import/Export** it is obviously that the USA import very small amounts of corn, this indicate that import is not crucial and they fully relay on domestic production. Although import volumes are low, they may contribute to supply diversification. Export is way higher than import, but it is not very stable and high which indicates that export is not a number one priority, but instead using it for food and industrial processing.



**Final Insights**

* **Corn production has increased significantly during last 25 years, which is the result of growing industry needs.**
* **The USA almost completely relay on domestic production, they managed to have stable and growing production and to be self-sufficient.**
* **Stable ending stocks suggest production is closely aligned with consumption needs.**
